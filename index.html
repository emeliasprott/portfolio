The Causal Effect of Twitter User Suspensions

This research project investigates how Twitter's suspension policies affect user behavior and platform dynamics. Through a comprehensive analysis of user data before and after suspensions, we explore the effectiveness of these measures in reducing harmful content and their impact on broader conversation patterns.

How COVID-19 Changed Migration

This study examines the profound shifts in migration patterns resulting from the COVID-19 pandemic. By analyzing demographic data across urban and rural areas, we identify emerging trends in relocation preferences and their implications for housing markets, infrastructure needs, and community development.

The Bay Area and the 2008 Housing Market Crash

This project investigates the impact of the 2008 recession on the Bay Area's economy and housing market. We examine how median home values, rates of home-ownership, and the total county property tax collection changed over time. We find that, despite an overall decrease in property values and home-ownership, aggregate property taxes increased. We discuss these implications in the context of Proposition 13 and the Bay Area's housing crisis.

Decoding the State Legislature: Partnership with the California Research Bureau (ongoing)

In collaboration with the California Research Bureau, this project uses natural language processing to analyze legislative documents and proceedings. Our goal is to increase transparency and accessibility of the legislative process for both policymakers and the public through interactive data visualizations and simplified summaries.
